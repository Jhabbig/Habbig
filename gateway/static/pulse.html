Methodology

What the Pulse Index is. A weighted composite of 16 wellbeing metrics normalized to a 0–100 scale across the longest available historical window. Each metric's weight is published in the data file /_gateway_static/pulse_data/pulse_metrics.json.

How forecasts are made. Each chart's forecast is a transparent statistical extrapolation, named on the chart itself. Most use linear regression on the last 15–25 years of data. Confidence intervals widen with horizon. Demographic forecasts cross-reference UN World Population Prospects 2024 where applicable.

Where the data comes from. v1 historical values are curated from public sources: CDC NCHS, US Census Bureau, GSS (General Social Survey), Gallup, Pew Research, Federal Reserve SHED, NIMH, World Happiness Report, YouMail Robocall Index. Each chart card cites its specific source. v1.1 will replace curation with live API pulls (FRED, Census, BLS) where available.

What the “What this moves” cards mean. Each downstream metric is paired with a historical correlation, lag estimate, and direction of effect. These are observed historical associations, not causal claims. narve Pulse does not provide investment, financial, medical, or legal advice.

Update cadence. The dataset is refreshed quarterly. Each chart displays its underlying source's freshness ceiling. Some macro series (CDC, GSS) have an unavoidable 1–2 year reporting lag.

Roadmap. v1.1 — automated quarterly refresh script + freshness badges. v2 — out-of-sample backtests on every forecast. v3 — proprietary structural models for the Pulse Index, replacing simple extrapolation.

narve Pulse is a forecasting product based on public statistical data. It is not financial, investment, medical, or legal advice. Forecasts are uncertain. Past correlations do not guarantee future relationships.
