1. At a glance

  • We collect only the personal data we need to run the Service and comply with law.
  • We do not sell or share personal data for advertising.
  • We do not run Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, behavioural cookies, or session-replay scripts.
  • We use essential cookies for authentication and CSRF protection. No consent banner is required because essential cookies are exempt under ePrivacy.
  • You can export, correct, or delete your data at any time from /settings or by writing to {{ privacy_email }}.
  • We respect Global Privacy Control signals as opt-outs.

narve.ai is the controller of personal data processed through the Service (except where we act as a processor on behalf of business customers — see our Data Processing Agreement).

3. Scope and key definitions

This Policy covers personal data processed when you visit our websites, use the Service, talk to our support team, open our emails, install our browser extension, receive our push notifications, or sign up for our newsletters.

Defined terms in this Policy track the GDPR / UK GDPR (controller, processor, data subject, personal data, processing, special category data). Equivalent terms apply under the CCPA ("business", "service provider", "personal information"), LGPD (controlador, operador), PIPEDA, APPI, DPDP Act, and similar laws.

4. Personal data we collect

4.1 Account data

  • Email address (required for all accounts).
  • Display name / username (optional).
  • Hashed password (PBKDF2 with per-user salt; we never store the plaintext).
  • Account creation timestamp, subscription tier, plan status, billing period dates.
  • Session tokens (HTTP-only cookies, hashed server-side), CSRF tokens, API keys (hashed).

4.2 Billing data

  • We use Stripe to process payments. We never see or store full card numbers, CVVs, or full expiry dates.
  • We store: Stripe customer ID, last-4 digits and brand of your card (for statements), billing address, subscription status, invoice identifiers, amount paid, currency.
  • Where required for tax purposes (EU VAT, UK VAT, US sales tax nexus) we store your jurisdiction and tax ID.

4.3 Usage telemetry

  • Pages viewed, features interacted with, counts and timestamps.
  • Predictions you saved, sources you followed, markets you watched, feedback you gave.
  • API usage (per key: endpoint, status code, latency, quota state).

4.4 Device & connection

  • IP address (for rate-limiting, security, geolocation for VAT; stored briefly in logs).
  • User-Agent string (browser & OS family, not a fingerprint).
  • Request-scoped headers needed for routing and CSRF validation.

4.5 Support communications

  • Emails you send us, including the address you send from and any content you include.
  • Feedback submissions, bug reports, feature requests.

4.6 Customer-generated content

  • Takes, comments, votes, watchlists, saved predictions, market annotations, survey responses.
  • Any content you attach when using the feedback or market-suggestion forms.

4.7 Notification preferences and delivery data

  • Which notification types you opted into (email, push, in-app).
  • Web-push subscription endpoints (for the browser you granted permission on).
  • Email send/open events where transactional deliverability monitoring requires it (we do not track newsletter opens for marketing).

4.8 Observability

  • Error events captured by Sentry (anonymised stack traces, no user-supplied content included by default).
  • Application logs (request metadata, status codes).

4.9 Integration data

  • If you link a prediction-market venue account (Polymarket, Kalshi), we store the OAuth / API credentials encrypted at rest, and read-only market positions for the features you enabled.

4.10 Special categories

We do not intentionally collect special categories of personal data (health, religion, sexuality, biometrics, genetics). Do not submit such data through forms or uploads.

5. Sources of personal data

  • Directly from you — registration, settings, feedback, integrations.
  • From your devices — standard HTTP headers.
  • From Stripe — billing events, invoice data.

12. Security

We maintain organisational and technical safeguards appropriate to the risk, including:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+), encryption at rest for secrets.
  • PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 password hashing with per-user salts and high iteration counts.
  • API keys stored as hashes only; shown once at creation.
  • CSRF tokens on all state-changing requests; SameSite=Lax cookies.
  • Rate limiting, IP-based abuse detection, and automated blocklisting.
  • Content-Security-Policy, Strict-Transport-Security, Referrer-Policy, Permissions-Policy, Cross-Origin-Opener/Resource policies.
  • Secret-scanning in source control and CI.
  • Role-based access control for admin surfaces; audit-logging of admin actions; impersonation sessions are separately logged.
  • Regular dependency updates, periodic vulnerability scanning, and responsible-disclosure channel.
  • Backups encrypted and region-restricted; backup retention matches the data retention table above.

No security measure is perfect. We will notify affected users and, where applicable, competent authorities of personal-data breaches in accordance with GDPR Art. 33/34 (within 72 hours to the supervisory authority), UK GDPR, and analogous laws.

13. Automated decision-making & profiling

Core features of the Service rely on machine-processed analysis of publicly available posts to produce credibility scores. These scores are not used to make decisions producing legal or similarly significant effects concerning you as a user. We do not use automated decision-making within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR to make decisions about you (such as refusing service or setting prices).

26. Social media posts we ingest for credibility scoring

To produce source credibility scores we periodically fetch publicly visible posts from third-party social platforms (e.g., X, Bluesky). We process:

  • The author's public handle (e.g., @alice).
  • The public post text and timestamp.
  • The public engagement counts where available.

We do not collect private messages, non-public content, or build consumer profiles on authors beyond features needed to score their historical accuracy at predicting event outcomes. Processing is based on Art. 6(1)(f) legitimate interest, supported by Art. 85 GDPR journalistic / research derogations as transposed in national law.

Authors have the right to object (Art. 21) to having their public posts included in our credibility data. Contact {{ privacy_email }}. We will remove scored data for an identified account within 30 days absent overriding compelling legitimate grounds.

Raw post content is deleted after 30 days; only de-identified aggregate features (accuracy ratios, calibration metrics) are retained longer, and those are not used to identify the individual.
