How It Works — Glossary

Edge %
The percentage difference between the sportsbook consensus probability and the Polymarket price. A positive edge means Polymarket is cheaper.
Confidence Score (1-5)
How confident the system is in this signal, based on book agreement, volume, spread, and time to event.
Book Agreement (1-5)
How closely the sportsbooks agree with each other. 5 = near-unanimous consensus.
Sharp Book
The bookmaker with the sharpest (most accurate) odds, weighted by market reputation.
Implied Vig
The bookmaker margin (overround) baked into the odds. Lower vig = cleaner probability estimate.
True Prob (No Vig)
The de-vigged consensus probability — the market's best guess at the true probability.
Book Range
The spread between the highest and lowest bookmaker probabilities for an outcome.
Median Book Prob
The median probability across all bookmakers, more robust to outliers than the mean.
Kelly Bet Size
Suggested bet size as % of bankroll using the Kelly Criterion, based on edge and probability.
Vol/Liquidity Ratio
Polymarket volume divided by liquidity. Higher ratios indicate more active trading relative to available depth.
Spread %
The bid-ask spread on Polymarket as a percentage. Tighter spreads mean lower execution cost.
Edge Direction
BUY if Polymarket is underpriced vs books; SELL if overpriced.
Time to Event
Hours until the event starts. Edges closer to event time are more actionable.
Best/Worst Odds
The best and worst decimal odds offered across bookmakers for the top outcome.
Match Confidence
How well the Polymarket question matched to the sportsbook event (string matching score).
Kalshi Price
The Kalshi prediction market mid-price for an outcome. Kalshi is a US-regulated prediction exchange (CFTC). Comparing both Polymarket and Kalshi gives you two independent market signals.
